Welcome to The Thomas Lab

Working on Host-Pathogen Interactions in Escherichia coli and Vibrio parahaemolyticus

Type III Secretion Systems

Both model pathogens, E. coli and V. parahaemolyticus utilize type III secretion systems as part of their pathogenic program. To understand how these molecular weopans function to aid in infection and cellular damage, we employ powerful microbial genetics tools with standard biochemistry.

The Enteropathogenic E. coli Multi-cargo Chaperone CesT

Of particular interest to type III secretion chaperones, CesT acts to carry multiple effectors to the secretion system. How it acts to do this, and whether the effectors have a heirarchy, or preference, for CesT association remains unclear, and is the focus of current Thomas Lab Research.

The Cytotoxic Type III Secretion System-1 of V. parahaemolyticus

Vibrio parahaemolyticus, an emerging zoonotic pathogen, is able to cause rapid cell death using a type III secretion system on it's first chromosome. Although the links to pathogenicity are not completely clear, what is clear is that this molecular weapon is a powerful tool against human cells. Focusing on how T3SS-1 is acivated, we aim to outline how T3SS-1 performs in Vibrio parahaemolyticus borne gastroenteritis.
